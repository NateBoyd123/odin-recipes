Baked Mac and Cheese

Description

Baked mac and cheese is a classic comfort food that is loved by many. The dish is made by combining macaroni pasta with a creamy cheese sauce, and then baking it in the oven until the top is golden brown and crispy. The cheese sauce typically contains a combination of cheddar cheese, butter, milk, and flour to create a rich and savory flavor. Some variations of the recipe may include additional ingredients like breadcrumbs, bacon, or even vegetables like broccoli or spinach.

One of the great things about baked mac and cheese is its versatility. It can be served as a side dish or a main course and is perfect for any occasion, from family dinners to potluck parties. Whether you prefer a classic recipe or like to experiment with different cheese combinations and mix-ins, baked mac and cheese is sure to satisfy your cravings for something warm, cheesy, and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni or other short pasta
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 4 cups grated cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Note that this is just a basic recipe and there are many variations you can make by adding different cheeses or mix-ins like bacon, ham, or vegetables. Also, feel free to adjust the seasonings to your liking.

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until smooth and bubbly.
  4. Gradually add the milk to the saucepan, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Stir in the Dijon mustard, garlic powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using).
  5. Cook the sauce, stirring frequently, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in 3 cups of the grated cheddar cheese until melted and smooth.
  7. Season the cheese sauce with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir to combine.
  9. Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to the greased baking dish.
  10. Sprinkle the remaining 1 cup of grated cheddar cheese over the top of the mac and cheese. If using, sprinkle breadcrumbs on top of the cheese.
  11. Bake the mac and cheese for 25-30 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the top is golden brown and crispy.
  12. Remove the mac and cheese from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. If desired, garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
