Coq au Vin with Garlic Mashed Potatoes and Roasted Vegetables

Description

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that consists of chicken cooked in a red wine sauce with vegetables and bacon. This complex dinner idea involves braising chicken thighs and drumsticks in red wine and chicken broth for an hour until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender. The dish is then enriched with diced bacon, sautéed onions, garlic, carrots, and celery, which add layers of flavor and complexity to the dish. The sauce is thickened with a tablespoon of tomato paste and simmered for another 10-15 minutes until it reaches the desired consistency.

To complement the rich flavors of the Coq au Vin, this meal is served with garlic mashed potatoes and roasted vegetables. The mashed potatoes are made by boiling potatoes until they are fork-tender, then mashing them with butter, cream, and roasted garlic for an extra depth of flavor. The roasted vegetables can be any combination of seasonal vegetables, such as brussels sprouts, carrots, and parsnips, tossed with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roasted in the oven until caramelized and tender. The combination of tender braised chicken, rich red wine sauce, creamy garlic mashed potatoes, and roasted vegetables creates a truly delicious and sophisticated meal that is perfect for a special occasion or a weekend dinner with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 6 chicken thighs
  • 6 chicken drumsticks
  • 8 ounces thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme leaves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Garlic mashed potatoes (potatoes, butter, cream, roasted garlic)
  • Roasted vegetables (such as brussels sprouts, carrots, and parsnips)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pot.
  3. Add the olive oil to the pot and heat over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs and drumsticks with salt and pepper, then add them to the pot and cook until browned on all sides, about 6-8 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onions, garlic, carrots, and celery to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the red wine, chicken broth, thyme leaves, and bay leaf to the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Return the chicken and bacon to the pot and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Cover the pot and transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 1 hour or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  7. While the Coq au Vin is cooking, prepare the garlic mashed potatoes. Boil potatoes until fork-tender, then mash them with butter, cream, and roasted garlic until smooth.
  8. Prepare the roasted vegetables by tossing them with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roasting in the oven until caramelized and tender.
  9. Once the Coq au Vin is done, remove the pot from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. Serve the chicken with the red wine sauce, garlic mashed potatoes, and roasted vegetables.

Note that the garlic mashed potatoes and roasted vegetables are prepared separately from the Coq au Vin and are not included in the steps for making the dish.
